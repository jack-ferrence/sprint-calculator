Sprint Rep Time Calculator

How to Use

Enter the rep distance (in meters) that you want to calculate a practice time for, and the target intensity as a percentage (e.g., 85 for 85%).

Then enter the athlete's known sprint times in seconds for as many of the distances listed as possible. Leave any unknowns blank.

Click "Calculate Rep Time" to estimate how long the rep should take at the given intensity.

How This Works

This tool uses a power-law regression model to fit a curve through the athlete's known sprint times. The formula is:

T(D) = A Ã— D^c

Where:

  • T(D) is the predicted time for the rep distance
  • D is the rep distance
  • A and c are calculated using log-log regression from the input times
Once the full-effort time is estimated, it is adjusted by dividing it by the given intensity percentage (as a decimal). For example, at 85% intensity, the rep time is longer than the all-out time.
